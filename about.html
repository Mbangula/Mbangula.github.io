Lameck Mbangula Amugongo

PhD Researcher in Cancer Science (Quantitative Image Analysis), software developer & Tech enthusiast

I am pursuing a PhD in Cancer Science in the Radiotherapy related research (RRR) group, University of Manchester, under the supervision of Dr Eliana Vasquez Osorio, Dr Andrew Green, Dr David Cobben, Prof Marcel van Herk and Dr Alan McWilliam. My PhD is sponsored by DARA Big Data, a Newton Fund project that provides students from the AVN countries with skills in the field of data science. My research involves investigating image-based data mining methods to analyse how lung tumours change during treatment. The aim is to distinguish elastic and inelastic tumour changes. This information can be used to personalise treatment through the adaptation of radiotherapy. Personalised treatment will enable the delivery of better-targeted radiotherapy and reduce toxicity to surrounding tissue.
I have keen interest in machine learning, big data and software development.

Background

Born and bred in Oshakati, Namibia. I completed my undergraduate and Masters studies at the Polytechnic of Namibia, now Namibia University of Science & Technology.
